Now if you break it down by race - among the Chinese two and a half in ten have diabetes, among the Malays five in ten have diabetes, and among the Indians six in ten have diabetes. This is people aged 60 and above. So half of Malays in my age group will have diabetes, more than half of Indians in my age group will already have diabetes. So this is a very serious problem. And especially for the Malays and Indians, it is actually a health crisis. It's not an exaggeration. The challenge with diabetes is that in the early stages it is an invisible disease. You don't feel sick. There are hardly any symptoms. You may not even know that you have it. But if it is not treated over time it can become very serious. If you look at the top causes of death in Singapore, diabetes does not appear there, but actually many common causes of death can be traced back to diabetes - heart attack, stroke, kidney failure, underlying cause, predisposing them to diabetes. Because diabetes damages all these organs. You may also lose your limbs or go blind. And for men, if nothing else worries you, diabetes can make you impotent!
