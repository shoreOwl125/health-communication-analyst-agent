Managing your diabetes well before surgery helps you to have a smoother and safer operation and recovery. High glucose levels increase your risk of poor and slow wound healing, wound infection, other infections such as pneumonia, urinary tract infections or sepsis, prolonged hospital stay and feeling generally unwell and delayed recovery. Managing your diet diabetes during and after surgery will help prevent these complications. Well before your surgery you should talk to your GP about your current blood sugar control, reviewing your medications, optimizing your blood glucose level, getting more exercise, weight loss to bring you closer to a healthy BMI and addressing complications.
