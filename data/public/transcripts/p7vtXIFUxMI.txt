With chronic kidney disease, you may have to adjust your diet to manage the gradual loss of kidney function. As your kidney disease progresses, you may experience lack of appetite where you eat less and fatigue, which can be challenging. To make it easier for you, there were three important points to consider when it comes to your diet. Meet your protein and energy needs. Protein and amino acids are building blocks of your body and will help you maintain your muscle mass and repair tissue. Eating from high value sources such as meat, fish, poultry or eggs and eating the calories you need will help your body to better use protein. Remember that your protein needs may vary according to the stage of your kidney disease. Your care team will be able to help advise on this. Keep track of the minerals: phosphorus and potassium. Phosphorus is a mineral found in many foods such as dairy, processed foods, sodas, wholegrain cereals and nuts. Persistent high levels of phosphorus will make your skin itch and might increase the risk of mineral and bone disease. A medication called Phosphate Binder can help bind some of the phosphorus present in what you eat, but not all of it. This way, a much lower amount is absorbed into your blood. Healthy kidneys regulate the amount of potassium in the blood, making sure its levels are right. It is present in almost all foods, especially fruits and vegetables. For a patient with kidney failure, however, potassium levels may vary a lot. They can rise between dialysis sessions. And when they become either very high or very low, they can affect your heartbeat. Keep the flavor, but not the salt. Sodium, a compound of table salt is naturally present in small amounts in most non processed foods. It is also used to add flavor and preserve most processed foods. And on top of that, sodium is used by our bodies to control fluid balance. So if your intake of salt is high and sodium levels are also high, your body will trigger thirst and make you drink more. The better nourished you are, the healthier you will be. Your care team is here to help you enjoy a fulfilling life when it comes to diet
