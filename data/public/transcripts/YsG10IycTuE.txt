Hello! I am Dr. Pankaj Kumar Sonar. I am a consultant to the Liver Transplant Surgery Department in R N Tagore Hospital, Mukundapur, Kolkata. Today I’m going to discuss certain facts about liver transplant surgery. Through liver transplant surgery we give a new lease of life to terminally ill patients at the last stage of liver failure. This can be done in two ways. Either we transplant taking a part of liver of a brain-dead person or with a part of liver donated by a close relative of the patient. First we’ll discuss how we can take the organ of a brain-dead person. A brain-dead person is the one whose brain-stem function is no more intact for some reason or the other. Most common such cases are victims of road accidents or cerebral stroke whose brain stem functions are completely dead. In such cases, after taking permission of the guardian or relatives, we can transplant parts of their vital organs among several needy patients to save lives. Liver is one such organ. We can extract the entire liver from a brain-dead person and transplant it into patients suffering from terminal liver diseases. The second option is, living donor liver transplant. It means, transplanting a part of the liver of a living person into the patient. In most cases the donor is a close relative of the patient, like parents, son, daughter, brother, sister, husband or wife. Even secondary donors like in-laws, cousins, etc. can donate a part of their liver to the patient. Up to 60% of the donor’s liver can be extracted and transplanted into the patient. The remaining 40% in the donor’s body is sufficient o lead a normal life. The remaining liver grows `into full 100% within almost a year. Liver is an organ that has the potential to re-grow. This way living donor liver transplant can save the life of a terminally ill patient. The foremost among causes of end stage liver diseases is consumption of alcohol. Another cause that is emerging as an epidemic is fatty liver disease, also known as nonalcoholic steatohepatitis. In times to come, it is going to overtake alcohol consumption to become the foremost cause of end stage liver disease. Even Hepatitis - B or C can damage the liver. Hepatocellular cancer or in other words liver cancer is another cause of terminal liver disease. In such case a liver transplant might become necessary.
