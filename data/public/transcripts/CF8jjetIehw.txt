Prof. José L. Zambrano, MD : Cardiology is in a continuous evolution and in fact in terms of valvular heart disease, we are experiencing now an enormous evolution in terms of diagnostic, but also in terms of treatment. And I am sure that in the near future, in the next year, everything is going to be really different. Why I am saying that is because what we are still doing today, and it is not bad. It is a proper way to treat valvular heart disease if you have a severe lesion, and then you send the patient to surgery. Let us say in aortic valve stenosis, so the valve, the aortic valve, is not opening properly. Well, the correct method of choice is sending this patient at a certain time of the disease to the surgical department and the surgeon will remove your valve and will insert prosthesis. But what is the latest from the latest? Well, there is a new approach also which is what we call the TAVI procedure. TAVI implies that via your artery, the cardiologist inserts a valve and it is placed without opening your chest so is just perform the procedure in terms of the percutaneous approach. So we do not need to open your chest. We do not need to really to stop your heart. We do not need even to open your heart. So the procedure is done percutaneously. In addition, I will say not so well established as TAVI because TAVI today is performed in many, many different centers with a great experience, with follow-up of years, and in different countries. Well, in a more advanced or the next step is going to be the percutaneous approach of the mitral valve. I think it is not so well established as the TAVI, but is no doubt that the percutaneous approach for mitral valve intervention is going to be also a reality in the future.
