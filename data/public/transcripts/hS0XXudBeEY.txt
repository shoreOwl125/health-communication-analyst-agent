I'm a medical oncologist and I work at the Center for Cancer Research at the National Cancer Institute in Bethesda, Maryland. The gastrointestinal malignancies team looks for novel treatments for gastrointestinal cancers, specifically hepatocellular carcinoma nd other types of liver cancer. Our research centers in liver cancer. We understand liver cancer and how difficult this journey can be. The team uses various approaches. In our protocols in which we are studying new treatments for liver cancer, we use novel combinations of medications. We also combine a drug that has been approved for liver cancer with one that has not. We use immunotherapy, which are drugs that rev the immune system of the patient to react against the cancer. We also use cellular approaches such as CAR-T treatments in which T-cells are collected from the patients. They are genetically engineered in the lab and then they are reinfused into the patients to attack the cancer. Let's go over some frequently asked questions. Do the clinical trials cost anything? Clinical care in the CCR is free and we also help financially with travel and other expenses. Who is eligible? Most of the patients that enroll in our trials have already received standard of care first-line treatment. Can I call if I just have questions? You can reach out to us for information, for a second opinion, for you, a loved one, or a family member, and we are always very happy to then reach out ourselves to your oncologist, as well.
