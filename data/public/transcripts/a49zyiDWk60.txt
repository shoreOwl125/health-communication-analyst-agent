The majority of the breast cancers are not associated with any risk factors. We call it sporadic breast cancer - it happens out of nowhere and it has absolutely no respect o men, women, you know, ethnicity, socioeconomic status to majority of the time, and therefore it can involve any patient. Being a woman, being older-age woman, and those women that they have a history of prolonged exposure to estrogen - early menarche, late menopause, the use of hormone replacement therapy, prolonged use of contraceptive pills - these are all associated with an increased incidence of breast cancer. They are secondary factors such as smoking, alcohol, obesity, these are all contributing factors for those patients that have some predisposition to breast cancer. But the most important risk factor for breast cancer is genetic mutation. Patients that have breast cancer genes, they have up to 55 to 85 percent more chance of development of breast cancer. Patients that have a strong family history of breast cancer, patients that have history of bilateral breast cancer in earlier age, patients that have male breast cancer in the family, Ashkenazi Jewish family, these are all people that they have more predisposition to breast cancer.
